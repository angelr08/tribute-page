Tim Berners-Lee

The man who communicated a billion people.

Tim Berners Lee working

Here's a time line of Tim Berners-Lee's life:

  • 1955 - Born in London, United Kingdom.
  • 1976 - Graduated in Physics from Queen's College, University of Oxford.
  • 1980 - Worked at CERN.
  • 1989 - Participated in the creation of the World Wide Web together with the first web server httpd (HyperText Transfer Protocol daemon).
  • 1994 - Entered the Laboratory of Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and launched the W3C.
  • 1998 - Get started on the semantic web.
  • 2002 - He received the Príncipe de Asturias award.
  • 2012 - Becomes the President of the Open Data Institute.
  • 2016 - He received the Turing award and proposed the possibility of distributing the content of the network through blockchains.
  • 2018 - Proposed Solid, an open source project that sought to give users complete control of the data.

“The web is more of a social creation than a technical one. I designed it for a social effect - to help people who work together - and not as a technical toy. The ultimate goal of the web is to support and improve our existence in the world wide web.”

-- Tim Berners Lee.
